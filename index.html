Speaking of « getting to the point », that’s a space where I can brag a little bit and talk about me. Let’s get into that. If you’re in a hurry, you can just check my resume here.

So, I studied computer science in the lovely city of Louvain-la-Neuve, in Belgium. After completing my Bachelor degree at EPHEC, I tried the computer science Master at UCL, that I eventually never finished. Don’t get me wrong, I really liked machine learning, but the appeal of startups and design was stronger!

After a quick trip around Europe, I move to Brussel and joined Skwarel, a startup which goal is to make accounting sexy (again?). Yeah, You read that right! During those amazing 3 years there, I really got the opportunity to cut my teeth on the Front-end side of things, and I eventually became the lead Front-end of the team. Oh, by the way, they are recruiting.

My next challenge was abroad, in Vietnam precisely. I got the chance to volunteer for an amazing NGO called Passerelles Numériques. They provides education and IT training to young underprivileged people in order to build their employability and allow them to escape poverty in a sustainable way. Pretty tight no?

My role their was to mentor 3rd year students, during their final project in which they were allowed to build a business project, using whatever technology stack they wanted. So there was a lot of task management, mockups, debugging and other such fluffy stuff that the tech startup life offer. And it was amazing! You can help them by donations, but they are also recruiting.
